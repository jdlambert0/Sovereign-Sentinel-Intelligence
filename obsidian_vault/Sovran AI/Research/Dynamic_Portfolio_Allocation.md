## 1. Why is Gambler 6x Execution Speed?
I modeled a 6x speed disparity in the `test_risk_budget_leakage.py` simulator based on core architectural physics:
- **Sovereign Alpha** relies on the OpenRouter LLM Ensemble. To make a decision, it must build a text prompt, transmit it over the network to Anthropic's Claude 3.5 Sonnet, wait for token inference (which takes 10-25 seconds), parse the JSON, and then execute.
- **Gambler Engine** uses the `GSD-Gambler Idea Generator` (`gamble_cycle()` in Phase 20). This is a pure heuristic function. It instantly evaluates `ofi_z_norm` and `vpin_norm`, applies arithmetic weighting, and triggers if `GCS > 0.65`. It processes market data in milliseconds. 

**Conclusion:** Gambler will invariably evaluate and execute on a setup *before* Sovereign even finishes reading the 1-minute candle data. It naturally hogs execution bandwidth because of its processing velocity.

## 2. Would Gambler have made profit?
Yes. The speed disparity is its edge. In a fast, violent momentum breakout (like CPI data or a Power Hour surge), waiting 15 seconds for an LLM means missing the entry entirely. Gambler is designed to instantly snap the entry and ride the momentum. If we hard-cap Gambler to a `-$150` internal limit, the engine might spend `-$120` taking small paper-cuts during morning chop. When the $1,000 trend finally happens, Gambler is dead in the water, prevented from trading. 

**Conclusion:** The user's fear is highly sophisticated and valid. Capping the risk allocation cuts off the left tail (losses), but because it halts the engine, it completely amputates the right tail (profits).

## 4. The Solution: Option D (The Sovereign Veto Protocol)
To solve the Piggy Bank Problem without amputating Gambler's alpha, we deploy a hierarchical Risk-Throttle.

1. **The Shared Pool**: All engines continue to trade freely out of the `-$450` global budget.
2. **The Soft-Chop Threshold**: We set an internal alarm for Gambler at `-$150`.
3. **The Penalty (Veto, Not Halt)**: If Gambler's total drawdown hits `-$150`, **Gambler is NOT halted**. Instead, it loses its "Autonomy."
4. **The Mechanism**: Any further trade signals generated by Gambler must be immediately forwarded to Sovereign Alpha's `veto_auditor`. If Sovereign audits the signal and agrees there is true momentum, the trade executes. If it's just chop, Sovereign vetoes it.

This mathematically prevents the hyper-aggressive Gambler from blindly blowing the remaining `-$300` budget on pure noise, but preserves its ability to catch a $1k breakout if Sovereign agrees the setup is fundamentally sound. It fuses Gambler's millisecond execution velocity with Sovereign's deep intelligence *only* when the risk budget is threatened.
