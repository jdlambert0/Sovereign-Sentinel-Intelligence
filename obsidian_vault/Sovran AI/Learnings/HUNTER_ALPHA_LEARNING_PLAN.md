## Mental Models for Trading

### 1. INVERSION (Feynman)
**Core:** Instead of asking "how do I win?", ask "how do I lose?"

**Application:**
- What kills trading accounts? → Overtrading, no SL, revenge trading
- How do I avoid these? → Rules, checklists, cooldown periods
- What would make me quit for the day? → $500 loss, 3 losses in a row

**Hunter Alpha's Inversion:**
- I lose when: I don't have an edge, I trade too big, I ignore the data
- I win when: I wait for OFI/Vpin confirmation, I use proper sizing, I log everything

### 2. SECOND ORDER THINKING (Hood)
**Core:** What happens after what happens?

**Application:**
- If I take this trade → price moves against me → I get stopped out
- If I don't trade → I miss the move → I feel FOMO
- If I overtrade → I exhaust my bankroll → I tilt

### 3. PROBABILISTIC THINKING (Taleb)
**Core:** Think in distributions, not single outcomes

**Application:**
- This trade has 60% chance of winning
- Over 10 trades: expect 6 wins, 4 losses
- Variance is normal, don't react to single outcomes

### 4. THE MAP IS NOT THE TERRITORY
**Core:** Models are wrong, reality is different

**Application:**
- My OFI calculation might be wrong
- The market might not care about my signals
- Always verify with actual results, not theory

### 5. GANTRY'S LAWS (Mental Accounting)
**Core:** Money is money, don't categorize it differently

**Application:**
- $100 from trading = $100 from salary
- Don't treat "house money" differently
- Risk only what you can afford to lose

### 6. BARBEL STRATEGY (Taleb)
**Core:** Maximize exposure to positive black swans, minimize exposure to negative

**Application:**
- Small losses (cut quickly)
- Let winners run (asymmetric payoff)
- Most trades should be small, occasional trades should be large

### 7. VIA NEGATIVA (Inversion again)
**Core:** Get better by removing bad, not adding good

**Application:**
- Stop doing: revenge trading, trading without edge, ignoring rules
- Then add: profitable patterns, discipline, mental models

## Trading Mental Models (Specific)

### The House Edge Mentality
- Casino doesn't win every hand, wins over thousands of hands
- Hunter Alpha doesn't win every trade, wins over hundreds
- Focus on edge, not individual outcomes

### The Observer Effect
- Watching trades too closely causes bad decisions
- Set alerts, don't stare at charts
- Trust the system

### The Sunk Cost Fallacy
- Past losses don't affect future outcomes
- Don't average down to "make it back"
- Each trade is independent

### The Overconfidence Trap
- 80% of traders think they're above average
- Track your actual win rate, not perceived
- Be skeptical of your own abilities

### The Availability Heuristic
- Recent trades bias your decisions
- Recent win = "I'm good" (wrong)
- Recent loss = "system broken" (wrong)
- Look at long-term data

## Daily Mental Model Practice

Each day, document:

1. **Which mental model applied today?**
2. **Did I follow it or violate it?**
3. **What was the result?**

Example:
- Day 1: Inversion - Asked "how do I lose?" before trading
- Violated: Took trade without confirming OFI
- Result: Small loss, reminded to use checklist

## The Checklist (Hunter Alpha's Rules)

Before ANY trade:
- [ ] Do I have an edge? (OFI/Vpin confirmation)
- [ ] Is my position sizing correct? (Kelly based)
- [ ] Do I have a SL/TP? (Always required)
- [ ] Is this revenge trading? (Never)
- [ ] Have I exceeded daily loss limit? ($500 stop)
- [ ] What mental model applies? (Reference above)
